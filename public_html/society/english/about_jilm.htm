About JILM

Outline of The Japan Institute of Light Metals
(１）Objectives:
　The Japan Institute of Light Metals (JILM) is a unique society for the scientific research activities on light metals such as aluminum, magnesium and titanium. The JILM was established in 1951 to promote the scientific and technological activities of the light metals field and to contribute to the continuous advance in the industries and has achieved variegated activities since the establishment.

(2) History:
1951　Establishment of the Research Society of Light Metals
1965　Reorganization of The Japan Institute of Light Metals
1991　40th Anniversary. International Conference on Recent Advances in Science and Engineering of Light Metals (RASELM ' 91)
1996　Corporation sanctioned by Monbu-daijin (Minister of Education, Science and Culture, Government of Japan)
1998　Organization of the 6th International Conference on Aluminum Alloys (ICAA6)
